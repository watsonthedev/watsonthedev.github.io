I finally crossed something off my ToDo list that has been there for over a decade.

I built an emulator of the Gameboy CPU (the Sharp LR35902) using C code, binary math, all wrapped inside a Swift app. Like seriously wtf. Have you read the GBA emulation docs? They are huge, intimidating, and dense (and of course amazing!).

I also recently started reading Atomic Habits by James Clear. In it he writes about something he refers to as "The Goldilocks Zone of skill building". Reading this it suddenly clicked why why it has felt so enjoyable and effortless to focus on these otherwise esoteric skills that I have struggled to make break into for so many years.

Claude keeps me in the Goldilocks zone. I'm not talking about agentic programming where I ask Claude to do all the work for me and it runs off and finishes the project on its own. I'm talking about a pair programming session with a tireless mentor who wants me to succeed and has all the collective information of the internet ready to be served up as soon as I start to stumble too hard.

It takes the project from Battletoads to Pokemon - difficult but not so impossible that you throw your controller (or Gameboy) after the first level.
